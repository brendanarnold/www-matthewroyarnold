SPIN STUDIES

About

The spins began in 2011 as abstract circular oil paintings on acrylic and glass discs and since then have developed through a series of studies involving a variety of media and contexts. Their abstract yet painterly nature serves as an ideal petri dish for wide ranging examinations into the perception and effects of art. In the Image Reaction Studies where the spins appear on pre-existing images, we examine how abstract shapes can obtain purpose and affect the mood of the underlying image. Similarly in the Place Reaction Studies spins on glass are arranged about a room in order to examine how we can exercise editorial control in how the room is perceived by the viewer. In contrast, the Taste Studies are deliberately kept abstract and instead focus on evolving a colour palette to the taste of the viewing public.

Place Reaction Studies

An examination of how a place or situation can be affected by abstract geometric shapes.

Image Reaction Studies

A further exploration of meaning of abstract shapes using pre-made images.

Taste Studies

An examination of preference in composition and palette of abstract geometric shapes.
